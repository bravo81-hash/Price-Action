Backtest — gist (5y event study, side-matched baselines)

Every rule was replayed over 5 years of history with the exact live scanner logic (pa_scanner/backtest.py, parity-verified against the live scanner) and scored against a seeded random baseline matched by side (long events vs random longs, short vs random shorts — so market drift can't masquerade as edge). ~60,000 events across three markets. Full per-slice tables live in backtest/report_<mkt>.md after a local run.

US — S3 (chop/quiet-name selection) is the validated edge.
  • S1/S2 directional entries: no aggregate alpha after drift correction (all rule×side |t|<1.6 at 10d).
  • Do not extend directional holds past ~21 days — excess turns significantly negative at 42–63d (S2 t=−4.6 to −5.4; longs alone −1.4%, t=−2.9). Part is vol/beta composition (signals cluster in high-ATR names), but the conclusion holds.
  • S3 quiet-name selection persists to 63d (11.3% vs 12.7% baseline abs move) — validates 30–60 DTE premium-selling tenors.
  • Bench-bearish stand-down is real and strengthens with horizon: −0.63% excess at 10d (t=−4.0) → −2.45% at 63d (t=−4.5). Dashboard shows a STAND-DOWN banner in this state.
  • evening_star retired as an S1 trigger — worst-of-side pattern in every market (US t=−6.0).
  • Score deciles are flat over 5y — score/rank are list organizers, not predictive.
India — the strongest directional result in the study.
  • S1 bearish EXIT flags are validated: +0.54% excess at 10d (t=2.99), strengthening to +1.07% at 63d (t=1.92) — treat as underweight-for-months, not a short-term trim.
  • S2 BUY (long) is a 6–13 week position trade: +0.95–1.14% excess at 42–63d (t≈1.8–2.3), favorable skew (med MAE −7.9% / med MFE +9.6%). These rows use the position exit template — stop 3.5×ATR, target 4.5×ATR, 63-bar time exit.
  • Everything else (S1 longs, S2 shorts) keeps the standard 10-bar swing template.
ASX — null at every horizon tested (10/21/42/63d).
  • No rule, side, or slice cleared significance after drift correction.
  • Remains a curated candidate screen on the standard 10-bar swing template — treat hits as watchlist generation, not a timing edge.
Candidate study — five documented-anomaly setups tested (promotion bar pre-registered: |t|≥2.5 at two adjacent horizons, or ≥2.0 replicated US+ASX):
  • OSMR promoted → live rule S4: oversold snapback in uptrend. US 5d excess +0.38% (t=3.44), ASX +0.34% (t=2.13), ASX persistence to 63d (t=2.31). Shallowest MAE of all candidates.
  • PBEMA near-miss (US 63d t=2.33, ASX 1.65 — below bar): parked in candidates.py for a future out-of-sample re-test.
  • NH52, HVOL, GAPD failed; candidate shorts were harmful everywhere (US all-shorts t=−3.9) — no short setups added.
  • RS≥70 conditioning replicated in both markets (t=2.26 / 1.96), reinforcing the RS layer.
  • Round 2 (OSMR family): STRK4 (4+ down closes) cleared the bar (US 5d t=3.17, ASX t=2.73 + ASX 21–63d persistence) and was folded into S4 as a second trigger. OSMR2 settled the parameter test — RSI(2) lost to RSI(3) in the US, so S4 keeps RSI(3)<15. LO7/BBMR/IBSMR failed. Replicated regime findings: bench-bearish supercharges MR (US +5.57% @63d t=9.37, ASX +4.09% t=6.96 → S4 exempt from stand-down and the counter-index penalty); ASX MR excess grows with horizon (→ ASX S4 position template); rich vol preferred (t=2.86/5.65).
Kept vs. changed (round-1 and round-2 decisions from the 2y and 5y runs):
  • Kept: S1 shorts (2y "collapse" was baseline drift, not a real effect — 5y side-matched shows |t|<0.8); India S2 (null, not negative — still generates candidates); score/rank as organizers.
  • Changed: s2_max_age 2→1 (age-2 entries negative in US+ASX); evening_star retired; side-matched baselines replace the original mixed baseline; exit templates (Stop/Tgt/time_exit columns) added from MAE/MFE distributions; India S2-long position template added from the horizon study.
  • Rejected: full score-weight rebuild (deciles flat over 5y — nothing stable to fit); fundamentals in scoring (no point-in-time history available via yfinance → unvalidatable, display-only if ever added).

How to use this app

Three tabs (US / ASX / India) show the day's scanner output for that market. Each row is a ticker where one of the three rules fired. Click column headers to sort; use the filter chips and the ticker search box to narrow the list; Export CSV downloads the current filtered view.

The three signal rules (all markets)

CodeNameWhat it looks for
S1Reversal at weekly level Price tests a weekly support/resistance zone (52-week pivots) with a daily reversal candle (engulfing, hammer, star). Requires the correct approach direction and a wick that actually tags the zone — a rally poking above resistance does not count as "support."
S2Trend pullback breakout Weekly trend (EMA-based) pulls back, then the daily close breaks the 7-day Donchian channel with volume confirmation. Must be fresh (≤1 bar old) and not already extended >1.5 ATR past the trigger — stale, chased breakouts are excluded.
S4Oversold snapback Long-only mean reversion above the 200SMA. Two backtest-promoted triggers: RSI(3) < 15 with 2+ down closes (round 1: US 5d t=3.44, ASX t=2.13), or a 4+ consecutive-down-close flush (round 2: US t=3.17, ASX t=2.73). US: 5-bar time exit. ASX: position template (3.5/4.5×ATR, 63 bars — excess grows with horizon there). Exempt from the STAND-DOWN banner: MR outperforms in bearish regimes (+5.6% US / +4.1% ASX excess @63d).
S3Range / chop (neutral) Low ADX, flat weekly EMAs, price mid-range, no boundary-pressing closes (which would mean coiling, not chopping). Validated in the backtest as genuine low-realized-move selection in all three markets — see the Backtest tab.

US tab — options playbook

Signals are enriched with vol context for short-premium selection, not used as standalone directional bets (the backtest found no aggregate directional alpha here).

  • Regime / Structure — weekly trend read (bullish/bearish/neutral) and the suggested options structure for that regime × signal combination.
  • Vol / IVR / IV / RV / VRP / Term — implied-vol context; IVR/IV are TWS-only (blank on the free yfinance path). Vol state (cheap/fair/rich) adjusts the S3 score.
  • Opt Liq — ATM open interest + bid/ask spread flag (OK/thin), TWS-only.
  • Live — real-time last-hour trigger status, only populated by --live runs.
  • STAND-DOWN banner — shown when the SPY benchmark regime reads bearish. Backtest- validated risk signal (−0.63% to −2.45% excess depending on horizon) — reduce size or stand aside while it's showing.
  • Hold window: keep directional theses inside ~10–21 days per the backtest. For 30–60 DTE premium selling, lean on S3 + rich vol, not directional S1/S2.

ASX / India tabs — long-only directional

No options, no shorting (matches the accounts these trade in). Each hit is tagged a single Action from weekly trend × trigger direction:

Weekly trendBullish triggerNo trigger (chop)Bearish trigger
UpBUY (add)HOLDREDUCE (trim)
FlatBUY (small)WATCHAVOID
DownWATCH (risky bounce)AVOIDEXIT (get out)

Green = add/hold long · amber = reduce/avoid/watch · red = EXIT. Conservative by design — a bullish reversal inside a downtrend is WATCH, not BUY.

  • India: EXIT/REDUCE flags are backtest-validated (strengthen with horizon — treat as underweight-for-months once triggered). S2 BUY rows carry the position exit template (stop 3.5×ATR / target 4.5×ATR / 63-bar time exit) — these are the app's one confirmed multi-week directional edge.
  • ASX: no measured timing edge at any horizon — treat hits as a curated watchlist, cross-reference your own TWS holdings before acting (this is a screen, not position-aware).

Score, Rank, RS

  • Score (0–1) — a heuristic composite from each rule's own inputs (pattern strength, trend alignment, volume, etc). Not shown to be predictive in the 5y backtest (score deciles are flat/inverted) — use it to compare candidates within one rule, not as a confidence number.
  • Rank — score's percentile within its own rule (0–100), so S1/S2/S3 hits sort together fairly in one list. Same caveat as Score.
  • RS — 63-day return vs the market benchmark (SPY/^AXJO/^NSEI), percentile across the scanned universe. Weak backtest support in the US (rs≤30 underperforms, t≈−1.8–3.0) — the most defensible of the three ranking columns, still a tiebreak rather than a standalone signal.

Stop / Tgt / time_exit

Every hit carries a suggested stop, target, and time exit, sized from the backtest's MAE/MFE distributions:

  • Standard (swing) template — all US/ASX hits, and India S1/S2-short: stop 2.0×ATR, target 1.5×ATR, 10-bar time exit.
  • India S2-long (position) template — stop 3.5×ATR, target 4.5×ATR, 63-bar time exit.
  • S3 (neutral) — stop/target show the range low/high as condor short-strike references.

Trade rules — quick reference

S1 — Reversal at weekly level (all markets · long & short · context, no measured aggregate alpha)
  • Setup: price testing a weekly S/R zone (52-week clustered pivots); approach direction confirmed — median of the prior 10 daily closes sits above the zone (support test) or below it (resistance test).
  • Entry: daily reversal candle on the test bar — engulfing, hammer/shooting star, piercing/dark cloud, morning star, tweezers (1-bar patterns need range ≥ 0.5×ATR). The wick must tag the zone (within 0.5×ATR) and the close must hold — no deeper than 0.5×ATR through it, no further than 1×ATR past it. Enter at/near the signal-day close (last hour).
  • SL: 2.0×ATR against entry (row Stop). PT: 1.5×ATR (row Tgt), or the next weekly zone if closer. Time exit: 10 bars.
  • Filters: counter-trend reversals score down; skip US names with earnings ≤ 10d for premium structures; obey the STAND-DOWN banner.
S2 — Trend pullback breakout (all markets · long & short · context in US/ASX; India longs validated at 42–63d)
  • Setup: weekly EMA10 > EMA30 and rising (mirror for shorts), with a pullback that touched the daily EMA20 within the last 10 bars; close back on the trend side of the EMA20.
  • Entry: daily close breaks the prior-7-day Donchian band, cross ≤ 1 bar old (Age column), extension ≤ 1.5×ATR past the trigger, breakout-bar volume ≥ 1.0× the prior-20 average. Enter at the close.
  • SL: 2.0×ATR. PT: 1.5×ATR. Time exit: 10 bars.
  • India S2 BUY (position trade): SL 3.5×ATR · PT 4.5×ATR · time exit 63 bars — the app prints these on the row.
  • US cap: never extend a directional S2 hold past ~21 days (42–63d excess is significantly negative).
S3 — Range / chop (neutral · US = premium selling; quiet-name selection validated in all markets)
  • Setup: ADX < 20, daily EMA20/50 flat (≤1.5% apart), range width 4–25% of price, price mid-range (30–70% position), not coiling (≤1 of the last 3 closes at a range edge), ≥ oscillation across the mid.
  • Trade (US): short premium — condor/calendar with short strikes referenced to the range edges (row Stop = range low, Tgt = range high). Prefer rich vol state and OK Opt Liq; 30–60 DTE tenors are supported by the data (quietness persists to 63d). Skip if earnings fall inside the tenor.
  • Exit / manage: exit or adjust on a daily close through either range edge ("broke"); otherwise manage to your premium plan and review at ~10 bars.
S4 — Oversold snapback (long only · all markets · backtest-promoted, both triggers)
  • Setup: close above the 200-day SMA (long-term uptrend intact).
  • Entry (either trigger): RSI(3) < 15 with ≥ 2 consecutive down closes, or ≥ 4 consecutive down closes regardless of RSI. Enter at the flush-day close (last hour).
  • US: SL 2.0×ATR · PT 1.5×ATR · time exit 5 bars — the edge is 3–10 days; do not linger.
  • ASX: position template — SL 3.5×ATR · PT 4.5×ATR · time exit 63 bars (excess grows with horizon there).
  • India: standard 2.0×ATR / 1.5×ATR / 5-bar time exit (same as US — only ASX gets the position template for S4).
  • S4★ PRIME: bench regime bearish → highest-conviction state (+5.6% US / +4.1% ASX excess @63d). Trade S4 normally (or up-size within plan) while the trend book stands down. Prefer higher-ATR% names — volatile names snap hardest (replicated).
  • Never short this or any setup here — candidate shorts were harmful in every market.
Cross-rule management
  • All entries assume the signal-day close; evaluate after the daily close (or via --live in the last hour).
  • STAND-DOWN banner = reduce/stand aside on S1/S2 trend entries only; S4 is exempt.
  • One bar spanning both stop and target counts as a stop (the ledger's conservative convention) — place real orders the same way.
  • Exit levels are printed per row (Stop / Tgt / time_exit) — no mental math at entry time.

S4★ PRIME

When the benchmark regime reads bearish, S4 rows are starred gold (S4★) and sort to the top — that cell is the single strongest result in the entire backtest program (+5.57% excess @63d, t=9.4 US; +4.09%, t=7.0 ASX, replicated). The stand-down banner applies to S1/S2 trend entries only; S4★ rows are the exception, not a contradiction. Filter chip: S4★ Prime. India is excluded (cell untested there).

Market state line + Qty (STFS-EQ imports)

The header shows a market state next to the benchmark regime — RISK_ON_CONTINUATION / RISK_ON_EXTENDED / PULLBACK_BUYABLE / CHOP_NO_EDGE / RISK_OFF — each with size guidance for the last hour (US uses SPY+QQQ+IWM+VIX+HYG; ASX/India use the benchmark only). It is context, not gating: only RISK_OFF overlaps a backtest-validated finding (the stand-down), and S4 remains exempt from it. Set risk_dollars in config.py (>0) to add a Qty column = risk ÷ stop distance per row.

Forward ledger

Every scan logs its hits into docs/data/ledger_<mkt>.json and tracks each one against its own stop / target / time exit on subsequent scans (conservative fills: entry at signal close; stop assumed first when one bar spans both levels). This builds true out-of-sample evidence for every rule — including the S4★ cell — and is the prerequisite for any future promotion round. Stats: python -m pa_scanner.ledger --market us — per-rule win% / avg win / avg loss / payoff / expectancy-per-trade, monthly drift buckets, S4-PRIME slice, and the oldest open positions.

Running a scan

Scans run locally (Python + TWS/yfinance) and push a JSON snapshot the dashboard reads. One-click runners: run_scan_all.bat (US + ASX daily, via TWS — the default), run_scan_live_us.bat and run_scan_live_asx.bat (each market's last hour is at a different clock time, so live runners are split — run US at ~3–4pm ET / ~5–6am AEST, ASX at ~3–4pm AEST / ~1–2am ET), plus the single-market run_scan.bat (US), run_scan_asx.bat, run_scan_india.bat. ASX trades via IBKR/TWS so it gets the same live last-hour refresh as US (S2 triggered/pending, S4 reclaimed/below-MA, S1 at-level); India is EOD-only (separate broker, no TWS). The cloud Action refreshes all three markets daily. run_backtest.bat regenerates the event-study reports.
